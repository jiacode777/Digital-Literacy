Case Study: UPI Online Payment Fraud – “Fake Payment Received” Scam
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, especially among college students. In this scam, fraudsters trick victims into believing they have received money by mistake and then ask them to return the extra amount.
How it typically happens (step-by-step):

The victim receives a WhatsApp message or phone call saying “₹5,000 has been accidentally credited to your account.”
The fraudster sends a fake UPI screenshot or spoofed message showing the credit.
They create urgency by asking the victim to immediately transfer back the “extra” amount to another UPI ID.
Once the victim sends the money, the fraudster disappears and the original “credit” never existed.

College students are the most targeted group because they frequently use UPI for food delivery, online shopping, small transfers, and recharges. In a realistic scenario, a first-year B.Tech student from VIT Bhopal lost ₹8,500 in March 2026 while ordering food online through a delivery app.
Consequences:

Immediate financial loss
Mental stress and anxiety
Loss of trust in digital payments
In some cases, identity theft if bank details are shared
Students often borrow money from parents or friends to recover the loss

This case clearly demonstrates how simple social engineering tricks can defeat even the most advanced UPI security features and highlights the need for awareness among young users.
